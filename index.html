Nanoscale Light-Matter

Welcome to my homepage! I am Sajal K. Giri, currently serving as a postdoctoral research associate at Northwestern University, USA, within the research group led by Prof. George C. Schatz. Our research interest involves solving, understanding, and controlling classical and quantum light-induced dynamical processes that take place in atoms, molecules, and nanoscale systems by developing theories, and implementing computational methods. A significant part of our research delas with the developement of machine learning (ML) methods to model the dynamics and spectroscopic aspects of such complex chemical processes.

Research

Machine Learning Light-Matter

The understanding of light-induced dynamics in molecules and nanoscale systems is often limited due to their inherent complexities. Are all these complexities essential that we take into consideration for solving the dynamics or provide enough transparency to gain insights? If there exists a subspace with minimal complexities encoding the essential dynamics then the problem is simplified significantly. But how do we explore that space if it exists at all? We combine quantum dynamics with machine learning methods to address this point.
